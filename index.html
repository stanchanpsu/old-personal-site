About

World, this is me. I'm a junior chemical engineering student at Penn State and that's awesome, but what else do I do outside the fume hood? Well, recently, I've found a hobby in programming and web development after auditing Harvard's CompSci class. So, to practice my new skills, I made this website to share some of me with some of you.

From outreach and mentoring, to karate, to peace & justice, there's always something that excites me. I've also created artificial photosynthesis, made toilet paper, and taught lego robotics. Above all, I am happy being happy. To me, happiness is being passionate, in the moment, and living every day like I'm going back in time to relive it on purpose. I hope that you always experience the same.

Blog

When in Doubt, /*out*/ is a tech blog created by me and Bryan Tan with the intention of helping us to become better programmers. He and I will be writing about our coding adventures in depth so that we and other people can reproduce our whacky creations.

Coding to me is a like an infinitely steep cliff with a few, very short plateaus. I climb and climb something seemingly unsurmountable, and I never think I can make it but then, finally I reach a spot where I can rest for awhile. Then, I want to implement a new algorithm or learn a new language and have to climb again. This blog is a place where I can write about those rare plateaus.
